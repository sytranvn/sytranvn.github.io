Vibe Writer

February 6, 2026 2-minute read
Sy Tran
AI • Vibe
The vibe coding term is quite popular now. But I think AI is not bringing only vibe coder but also vibe writer as well.

I have seen significant more posts on social networks like Facebook, LinkedIn written by AI. At the surface, they look charming, precise and detailed. But if we ask the authors without peaking at what they have posted, and tell what each thesis statement in it, we might be left hanging. Sometimes, the statements are short, we can keep asking them to elaborate with supporting arguments. They probably won’t tell us. No one read what AI wrote.

I like to call them vibe writers. I am one of them sometimes, when I have an idea but don’t want to explain my thought. Or sometimes my knowledge wasn’t enough to prove it, so I let AI do the heavy lifting job. I then skim through the content and post it immediately.

But in the end. I made this blog because I wanted to practice writing. So I can write better technical documents for my team, my company. Because I am so grateful one of my first project have so detailed documents. It helped me grow from a junior to more senior mindset. When I explored open source software documents, they are so easy to read and apply. And when I saw new comers suffer in spaghetti code without guidelines in another company, I realized my job isn’t just about coding. Most people ignore writing documents part because it’s daunting. The fear of people might see their stupid grammar mistakes or typos keeps them back. Code itself doesn’t lie, but if we don’t see the code in motion, we can’t see the full animation in action. Documents are the intros that let people know what it is about without having to watch the full series, or reading the full books. Of course, if they are interested, they still have to spend time for those series or books. But they know what to look for.
